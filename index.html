Medicinal Trees & Plants

Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera soothes burns and boosts skin health.

Aloe Vera is a succulent plant known for its gel-filled leaves. The gel is used topically to treat burns, wounds, and skin conditions like acne. It contains vitamins, minerals, and enzymes that promote healing and moisturize the skin.

Neem Tree

Neem is a natural antimicrobial and supports skin health.

The Neem tree, also known as Azadirachta indica, has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Its leaves, bark, and oil have antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties. Neem is effective against skin disorders, dental issues, and as a natural pesticide.

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Tulsi is known for its adaptogenic and immune-boosting properties.

Tulsi, also known as Holy Basil, is revered in Ayurveda for its medicinal properties. It helps reduce stress, supports respiratory health, and has antimicrobial effects. Regular consumption can boost immunity and improve digestion.

Ginger

Ginger aids digestion and reduces nausea.

Ginger is a powerful root with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It is commonly used to alleviate nausea, improve digestion, and reduce muscle pain. Ginger tea can help with colds and sore throats.

Turmeric

Turmeric has anti-inflammatory benefits due to curcumin.

Turmeric contains curcumin, a compound with strong anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. It supports joint health, aids in wound healing, and may improve brain function. Often used in golden milk for its benefits.

Mint

Mint freshens breath and aids digestion.

Mint leaves are excellent for soothing digestive issues, freshening breath, and relieving headaches. Peppermint oil can help with irritable bowel syndrome and muscle spasms. It's also used in teas for relaxation.
